HOW TO CHOOSE A TRAINER

Check Their Record

Verify NRHA earnings, Futurity and Derby placings, and competitive history through NRHA's ReinerSuite. A trainer's competitive record shows how their horses actually perform when it counts.

Visit the Facility

Tour the barn, inspect the arena footing, and observe how horses are handled day-to-day. A well-managed facility with quality footing, clean stalls, and calm horses tells you more than any website.

Understand Their Program

Ask about training schedules, communication style, client involvement, and how they handle young horses vs. proven competition horses. Make sure their approach matches your goals and timeline.

Talk to Their Clients

Ask for references from current Non Pro clients — not just owners. How a trainer communicates with clients and develops riders (not just horses) is critical to a successful long-term relationship.

Understand the Costs

Get a clear breakdown of monthly board, training fees, show entry costs, and veterinary/farrier protocols. Elite programs cost more but top trainers often provide better long-term value through horse welfare and results.

Match Level to Goals

A Futurity-focused trainer may not be the right fit for a Non Pro looking to show at affiliate shows. Find a trainer whose primary program aligns with your competitive goals and level of involvement.
